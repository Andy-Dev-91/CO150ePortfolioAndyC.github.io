Reflective Memo

This page presents my Reflective Memo (A4), which analyzes my growth as a writer throughout the course and reflects on the skills and strategies I have developed.

Memo Content

TO: Professor Annie Halseth

FROM: Andy Chrismer

DATE: December 16, 2025

SUBJECT: Reflective Memo on my growth as a student and writer

Dear Professor Halseth,

The purpose of this memo is to reflect on my growth as a writer and student throughout this semester in CO150. When coming into the course I had a limited understanding of how much writing depends on research and rhetorical awareness. Over the semester I learned that writing is not just about putting words on a page to complete an assignment, but about making writing choices based on my audience, purpose, and evidence. During this course and another class that required research and major writing assignments I developed more confidence in my writing skills and better understanding of my learning process.

The course outcome where I saw the most improvement was using sources and evidence, such as selecting and evaluating appropriate sources. Before this course I was not familiar with the process of properly citing a source or how to tell if a specific source was useful to my research. Previous research assignments felt overwhelming because I did not know how to properly read research articles. During Unit two and three I learned how to use the library databases to locate peer reviewed sources and decide if they supported my arguments. This course helped me to understand that research is about finding the right sources instead of finding as many sources as possible to support my claims. As a result of this class I feel more confident working on research based assignments. This course outcome is important for my future writing because many of my upcoming courses will require research papers, lab reports, and projects that need reliable evidence. In my day to day life these skills have helped me better evaluate sources of information such as news and videos online.

Another course outcome I engaged with and want to continue developing beyond this course is deepening my experience in writing, with a focus in generating ideas, editing drafts, and proofreading. I struggle at the start of large projects when it comes to generating ideas. I have also had a bad habit of focusing too much on minor things like grammar. The “Shitty First Drafts” reading by Anne Lamott helped me understand that I was wasting time attempting to have perfect sentence structure and that I should let my ideas flow freely and that there is always time for corrections later. Moving forward in my studies I want to improve my brainstorming and spend more time in the editing and proofreading process so I can further deepen my experience in writing

The reading from Bad Ideas About Writing that stood out to me the most was “You Can Learn to Write in General” by Elizabeth Wardle. In the reading Wardle explained that you needed an audience, a purpose and the context if you wanted to write anything. This idea helped me understand the information I will need for all my upcoming writing assignments.

This semester taught me a lot about myself as a student and writer. Most importantly, I need time to develop my ideas. Peer feedback can be a very useful experience and helps me see my work from a different perspective. As a student, I learned that I need to break my assignments into smaller steps and have better time management to help me feel less overwhelmed. This will change how I approach future assignments by forcing myself to start earlier.

In conclusion, this class helped me grow into a more confident and effective writer. I made the most progress in using sources and evidence, brainstorming is an area I will continue to develop. The skills I have learned in this course will continue to improve my academic career and I look forward to developing my personal career with these skills in the future.

Very Respectfully, Andrew “Andy” Chrismer
