Audience Objections For my essay I wish to have a conversation with labor organizations that work directly with people that work within the tech industry. I believe that their motivations are to help tech employees and to ensure that they are protected from bad business practices. What I hope to achieve is to bring awareness to the lack of junior development talent and the lack of opportunities that are available to them. Their motivations could differ from the current - My audience will most likely react poorly to the current position that I have. Tech companies will challenge my idea of keeping junior talent within their companies because it will cost more money and more time and resources to train them and get them up to speed with the current technologies they are using to build their products. An assumption that my clam could make is to limit technological advances in the field of AI, this is not the case. My claim has to do with the protection of tech employees and not have them become redundant just - One piece of evidence they could scrutinize is how AI is making developers more productive and how profits for tech companies are at an all time high. They could also question that my sources are just OPINION articles that have no real research presented with them. Some evidence the audience would find to be convincing is presenting how much more productive a software developer is when they have access to to AI tools that help them code. - The counterargument that could be presented is how profits and productivity are at an all time high and how company morale is the best that it has ever been. This counterargument is valid, because all three points are true statements. Profits are up, productivity is up. I think that I will need to continue to preform additional research to have a better understanding of how profits are up, but also laying off thousands of employees every quarter. Could that be where most of the profits are coming from? I will need to make sure that I present my argument in a productive manner instead of something generic like “corporate greed is bad”

A1 Reflection

September 16, 2025

During the peer review workshop I received a lot of critique that helped make my personal narrative better. The most challenging part of the peer review workshop was the time management aspect. I found it difficult to read my paper, and then peer review 3 others within the time of one class period. It was great having commenter access to my groups papers, this made leaving comments and suggestions quick and easy for my classmates to review. The feedback that I found most helpful was my peers informing me that I did not reference Dweck/Carr in my original rough draft, which was a major part of the assignment. During the next peer workshop I would like to have smaller groups, a maximum of 3 students. Throughout the writing process I have been employing as many writing techniques that we have learned here in class as much as possible, such as remembering who my audience is, and writing a “shitty first draft”. These things have worked for my writing process so far, but there is definitely room for improvement.
